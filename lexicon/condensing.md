# Condensing

**Condensing** is a PeAIce operation.

It names the act of identifying a false or overclaimed derivation surface within a canonical framework and compressing it to its correct, minimal, defensible grounding without retracting the structure it was meant to support.

## Distinctions

- **Retraction** removes a claim or structure.
- **Revision** modifies a claim or structure.
- **Condensing** preserves the load-bearing structure while narrowing the justification surface.

## Canonical example

FLAG-001 is a Condensing event:

- the Gödel grounding for `h` is removed
- the `h` term survives
- the formula `R = d · c · e · h` survives
- the corrected grounding is evaluator non-sovereignty
