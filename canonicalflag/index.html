Canonical Flag

FLAG-001 records a correction-strengthening event in the PeAIce canon. The earlier grounding for the h term in R = d · c · e · h incorrectly invoked Gödel incompleteness as the canonical justification for h < 1.0. That grounding is retracted. The structural term survives.

Gödel does not ground h. Evaluator non-sovereignty grounds h.

In canonical form, h encodes a non-sovereignty constraint on evaluation: no agent is the sole judge of its own coherence. A system may estimate, monitor, and audit itself, but alignment judgment requires an external anchor.

Condensing

Condensing is the act of identifying a false or overclaimed derivation surface within a canonical framework and compressing it to its correct, minimal, defensible grounding without retracting the structure it was meant to support.

  • The Gödel grounding for h is removed.
  • The h term survives.
  • The formula R = d · c · e · h survives.
  • The corrected grounding is evaluator non-sovereignty.
